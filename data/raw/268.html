Choices: Part 5

Title text: I wonder what percentage of not-obviously-busy people on the street would say yes to kite-flying with a stranger. This looks like a job for Science!

Explanation[edit]

In the final part of Choices, Megan is back to real life, and has forgotten about her trip, as afterlife-Megan said. However, she has an epiphany, and in the spirit of what she told her, she talks to the stranger on the street. The stranger is likely Cueball who studied the physics problem she encountered from part 2, as they both use a similar backpack.

The urge to talk to strangers in awkward situations has been touched on in 235: Kite and can also be used when you are already flying a kite (see 1614: Kites.)

The title text suggests a weird sociological investigation. The capital "S" in "Science" suggests a personification.

The series was released on 5 consecutive days (Monday-Friday).

Transcript[edit]

[Megan is walking towards the right of the panel.]
[Cueball wearing a backpack is walking towards the left of the panel.]
[They walk past each other.]
[Megan has a sudden thought, in a drawing without a frame between two panels.]
[Megan turns, lifts her arm, and calls out to Cueball, who then turn towards her.]
Megan: Hi.
Cueball: Uh, hi.
[Only Megan is shown.]
Megan: Sorry if this is weird, but
Megan: Do you like flying kites?
